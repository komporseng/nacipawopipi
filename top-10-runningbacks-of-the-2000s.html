    The 2000s marked an era of exceptional running backs in the NFL, with players who left a lasting impact on the game.

    From powerful runners to elusive playmakers, the decade showcased a diverse range of talents. Here, we present a list of the top 10 running backs of the 2000s, highlighting their remarkable contributions to the sport.

    1. LaDainian Tomlinson

    LaDainian Tomlinson, often referred to as "LT," was a dominant force throughout the 2000s. His exceptional vision, agility, and versatility made him a threat both as a runner and a receiver.

    Tomlinson's standout year came in 2006 when he set the single-season touchdown record with 28 total touchdowns, earning him the NFL MVP award.

    2. Shaun Alexander

    Shaun Alexander's impact in the early 2000s was undeniable. His powerful running style and impressive speed made him a cornerstone of the Seattle Seahawks' offense. In 2005, Alexander led the league in rushing yards and touchdowns, earning him the NFL MVP award.

    3. Edgerrin James

    Edgerrin James was a consistent performer throughout the 2000s, known for his durability and ability to contribute as a receiver out of the backfield.

    James achieved multiple 1,000-yard rushing seasons and played a crucial role in the Indianapolis Colts' offensive success.

    4. Marshall Faulk

    Marshall Faulk's impact extended from the late 1990s into the early 2000s. His exceptional pass-catching ability as a running back revolutionized the position. Faulk played a pivotal role in the "Greatest Show on Turf" with the St. Louis Rams, earning multiple Pro Bowl and All-Pro selections.

    5. Tiki Barber

    Tiki Barber's exceptional versatility as a dual-threat runner and receiver set him apart during the 2000s. His ability to evade defenders and contribute in the passing game made him a valuable asset for the New York Giants, where he consistently surpassed 1,000 rushing yards in multiple seasons.

    6. Priest Holmes

    Priest Holmes made a significant impact with the Kansas City Chiefs in the early 2000s. His remarkable production as a dual-threat running back, coupled with his ability to find the end zone, earned him three consecutive rushing titles from 2001 to 2003.

    7. Clinton Portis

    Clinton Portis was known for his explosive running style and ability to break big plays. His time with the Denver Broncos and later the Washington Redskins showcased his exceptional speed and agility, leading to multiple 1,000-yard seasons and Pro Bowl appearances.

    8. Brian Westbrook

    Brian Westbrook's dynamic skill set as a runner and receiver made him a valuable asset for the Philadelphia Eagles. Known for his elusiveness and versatility, Westbrook consistently posed a threat to opposing defenses, earning multiple Pro Bowl nods and All-Pro honors.

    9. Fred Taylor

    Fred Taylor's combination of power and agility made him a standout running back for the Jacksonville Jaguars throughout the 2000s. His ability to break tackles and gain significant yardage solidified his reputation as one of the top rushers of the decade.

    10. Ahman Green

    Ahman Green's exceptional speed and durability made him a key player for the Green Bay Packers during the 2000s. Green consistently surpassed the 1,000-yard rushing mark and played a vital role in the Packers' offensive schemes.

     

    The 2000s featured a remarkable cohort of running backs who brought diverse skill sets and playing styles to the NFL.

    From power runners to versatile dual-threat backs, these players left an indelible mark on the game and continue to be revered for their contributions to the sport.
