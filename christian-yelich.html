    Christian Yelich, whose full name is Christian Stephen Yelich is an American professional baseball player. Christian Yelich currently is an outfielder at Milwaukee Brewers of Major League Baseball (MLB) and he has field experience of 7 years.

    Early Life and Childhood

    Christian Yelich’s full name is Christian Stephen Yelich and he was born on December 5, 1991, in Thousand Oaks, California. He was brought up by his mother Acecie Yelich. However, there is no information about his father. Christian has 2 brothers Collin and Cameron.

    Christian Yelich’s maternal great-grandfather was American football player Fred Gehrke. One of his uncles, Chris Yelich, played football for the UCLA Bruins. Similarly, Christian is the oldest of three children. One of his brothers, Collin, was selected by the Atlanta Braves in the 2015 Major League Baseball draft.

    Also, Cameron is a United States Marine. His paternal grandfather was Serbian while his maternal grandfather was Japanese. Moreover, Yelich grew up as a fan of both the Los Angeles Dodgers and New York Yankees. Further, his current age is 30 years old and his zodiac is Sagittarius.

    Education

    Milwaukee Brewers’ outfielder Yelich attended Westlake High School for education. Since his high school days, Yelich played baseball for the school team. He started baseball in his freshman year and kept on growing better every year. As a senior, he batted 451 in  37 hits, 9 strikeouts, and 8 home runs in 82 at-bats.

    Consequently, Max Preps named him to the second-team All- American. Moreover, he was enlisted in the top 100 players in the nation in high school at 34th position. Impressed by his performance on the baseball field, the University of Miami offered him a scholarship to play collegiate baseball.

    Career and Professional Life

    Miami Marlins

    Although Yelich was offered a scholarship, he was drafted by the Marlins 23rd overall in the 2010 Major League Baseball draft. Yelich played six games for the Gulf Coast League Marlins. During this, he got nine hits and seven strikeouts with a batting average of .375. After that, he advanced to Class-A.

    Then in 2010, he played 6 games for the Greensboro Grasshoppers. He showed extraordinary performance in the 2011 and 2012 Marlins’ Minor League and was able to claim the player of the year in both years. In the following year 2013,  Marlins promoted Yelich to the major leagues from the Double-A Jacksonville Suns.

    His success kept on reaching new heights. in 2014 he became the franchise’s youngest-ever player and first outfielder to win Gold Glove Award. Impressed with his talents Marlins finalized a seven-year, $49.57 million contract extension with Yelich. But he had to struggle in the 2015 season due to his injury and ended with a .300 average.

    Milwaukee Brewers

    In the year 2018, the Marlins traded Christian Yelich to the Milwaukee Brewers. After batting .285 with 11 home runs, 36 RBIs, and 11 stolen bases he was named to the 2018 MLB All-Star Game. In the year 2018, Yelich hit his first career grand slam against the Washington Nationals. In the same year, he hit for the cycle, against the Cincinnati Reds and became the fifth player in MLB history to hit two cycles in the same season and the first player in MLB history to do so against the same team.

    The 2018 season ended with great achievements for Yelich. He finished it with a .326/.402/.598 slash line, 36 homers, and 110 RBIs, and even won the first NL batting title in Brewer’s history. But fell short of a triple crown with a narrow margin.

    On July 1, 2019, he broke Brewer Prince Fielder’s record of 29 home runs by making 30 home runs before the All-Star Break.  In 2019 he batted .329/.429 /.671  with 44 home runs, 11.1 at-bats per home runs, a .342 ISO, 30 stolen bases, and 97 RBIs in 130 games. This brought him his second National League batting title
