    The reigning and beloved Mr. Olympia Classic Physique champion, Chris Bumstead, is definitely one of bodybuilding and fitness's larger than life figures.

    Every bit of him from a zealous athlete to a celebrated icon has largely been affected by his mentor and brother in law, Ian Valliere.

    Chris Bumstead, who is known for his charming personality, and who deserves to share his life story with everyone is an inspiration to all the people out in the world, pursuing their fitness goals.

    Early Life and Background

    Chris Jonathan Bumstead was born on the 2nd of February, 1995 in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada. After achieving pro status at the IFBB North American Bodybuilding Championship at just 21, a career in bodybuilding ensued.

    Professional Career

    Bumstead exploded onto the scene in the Classic Physique division, right out of the gate earning a second-placing finish at the 2017 and again in 2018 Mr. Olympia competition.

    Nonetheless, his efforts and tenacity paved the way to his Mr. Olympia honors in 2019, 2020, 2021, and 2022, establishing him as a prominent force in the Classic Physique sector.

    Having won numerous titles and accolades throughout his competition career, he is considered one of the biggest names in the sport of bodybuilding.

    Enterprises and Social Media Influencing

    Aside from Bodybuilding, Bumstead is a co-owner of a supplement company, Raw Nutrition, and has his own clothing line, product line, and a training app subscription service.

    He's a popular athlete and he is an ambassador for fitness brands which shows how much he is loved and respected by the fitness community.

    Social Media Presence

    He now owns 1 million+ followers on Instagram, 500K+ subbies on YouTube, and 4.5M followers on TikTok as of August 2023; keeping him at the top of the list for the best, current social media following in the conversation of the highest following physique athletes.

    Even though he has already won the classic physique division, there have been rumors about Chris Bumstead moving up to the mens open division.
