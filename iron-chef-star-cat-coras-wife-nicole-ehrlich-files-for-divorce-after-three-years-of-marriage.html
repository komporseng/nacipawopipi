    Damian Lillard has reportedly filed for divorce just days after being traded to the Bucks in a blockbuster deal.

    Lillard had agitated for a move this offseason after 11 years in Portland, and was eventually sent to Milwaukee in a three-team deal involving Jrue Holiday, Deandre Ayton and Jusuf Nurkic.

    Now, it seems there will be another major life change for the point guard, as he’s filed for divorce from his wife Kay’La Lillard due to ‘irreconcilable differences,’ according to the Willamette Week.

    The couple, who met in college at Weber State University, wed in September 2021.

    Damian Lillard has reportedly filed for divorce from his wife Kay’La Lillard

    Lillard is seen with the couple’s three young children in a recent picture posted to Instagra

    The Bucks star and Kay’La are seen during a trip to the Coliseum in Rome, Italy

    Lillard filed for divorce on October 2 – just five days after his trade to the Bucks.

    The couple have three young children together.

    The filing obtained by the Willamette Week suggests that Kay’La moved into a $2.7million home in December 2022 – with Lillard staying in the couple’s $7.7million mansion.

    In a November 2021 Instagram post, when things were seemingly smoother between the pair, Lillard wrote a sweet message for his wife’s birthday. 

    ‘My life with you is so much better than my life without you. Thank you for being the wife you are…the mother you are…the friend you are… and the super solid person you are,’ he wrote in an Instagram post.

    Lillard arrives in Milwaukee to a welcome rally and tour on September 30 at Fiserv Forum

    Lillard will team up with Giannis Antetokounmpo (left), Khris Middleton (second right) and Brook Lopez (right) on the Bucks

    ‘You truly make me a better man and make me stand up taller! I go harder knowing that my work will continue to give you and my kids the world… you and me always baby… I love you.’

    Last Saturday, Lillard was welcomed by hordes of Bucks fans at a welcome rally in Milwaukee.

    He will team up with Giannis Antetokounmpo in Milwaukee, with the team expected to contend for another championship following its 2021 title win.

    The Bucks begin their preseason vs. the Bulls on October 8.
