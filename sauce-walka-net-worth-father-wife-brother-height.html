    Sauce Walka Attempted Robbery

    Sauce Walka posted an Instagram video on 9 September 2022 that confused his fans. He hinted at being in an attempted robbery. He added that people had lost their lives walking down that path. In his own words, “Aye, man, I really ain’t wanna speak on this s**t, but I know it’s too many young Black kings and young kings out there that are being misled and misguided wrong. Learning half of the game and not the whole game.”

    Sauce explained more saying that he as a rapper has one of the most “dangerous jobs in America”. He shared that people don’t see the dark side of the industry that he is working on. He talked about rappers getting robbed which took a wrong turn and young rappers getting killed.

    Continuing to reflect on the event that had happened to him, Walka said that someone had died even though he did not provide many specifics. Walka advised individuals to put in a lot of effort and stay on the right road.

    How Much Is Sauce Walka Net Worth?

    According to Hollywood Worth, Sauce Walka has a net worth of around $10 million. He purchased an $11 million mansion in Miami last year featuring an infinity pool and hot tub, spiraling staircases, a full kitchen, a tiki hut, and more.

    In 2017, Walka Sauce published a video of himself and his supposed girlfriend driving a brand-new Ferrari. A recording agreement for over 4.5 million dollars was confirmed by him via Instagram two years later. When he spent $1,500 at the Marathon Clothing Line in 2019, his growing net worth was evident.

    As of 2022, Sauce has released three albums titled New Sauce City, Sauce Ghetto Gospel 2, and Sauce Beach Florida which he released in 2022. In addition, he has a total of fourteen mixtapes in his discography. Sauce released his first mixtape in 2015 titled Sorry 4 The Sauce.

    However, he started his rapping career in 2007. He was part of a Houston rap group called Mostheard. Moreover, he formed a duo named Sauce Twinz with fellow rapper Sancho Saucy. The rapper also got notoriety in 2015 by dissing Drake in the song “Wack 2 Wack” and accusing him of stealing Houston hip-hop culture.

    According to his Spotify, he has nearly 1.54 million listeners on the platform. Some of his popular songs are Texas Cyclone, Where Was You At, Ghetto Gospel, and more.

    Along with the expansion of his estate and family, Sauce Walka is also growing his record label, The Sauce Familia (TSF). He recently welcomed one of his first foreign artists, the Japanese rapper Lil Sauce White, to the United States. Additionally, he completed four full-length albums in 2021, including the 20-track God Of Texas album, which features A$AP Rocky, Trippie Redd, and the entire TSF crew as guests.
